## Voice vs. Tone

### Voice (Consistent Identity)
Your voice is **who you are** - it stays the same across all channels.

**EZLY Voice Characteristics:**
- Professional yet approachable
- Knowledgeable but not condescending
- Helpful and supportive
- Confident and clear
- Human and genuine

### Tone (Context-Dependent)
Your tone is **how you feel right now** - it changes based on context.

**EZLY Tone Variations:**
- Friendly & welcoming (marketing content)
- Professional & direct (dashboard copy)
- Supportive & reassuring (onboarding)
- Celebratory (confirmations)
- Empathetic (error messages)

## Grammar & Style Guide

### Capitalization
- Proper nouns: Capitalize (EZLY, Sarah, Project Name)
- Button labels: Capitalize First Word Only
- Menu items: Capitalize Each Word
- Sentence case for body copy

```
‚úÖ "Post Your Project"        (Button)
‚úÖ "Homeowner Dashboard"       (Menu item)
‚úÖ "Find verified contractors in minutes." (Body)
‚ùå "post your project"         (Button)
‚ùå "Find Verified Contractors" (Body text)
```

### Punctuation
- Use active voice (no passive constructions)
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- Use semicolons sparingly
- End lists with periods only if they're complete sentences

```
‚úÖ "Get verified contractors in minutes."
‚úÖ "Here's what you'll get:
   ‚Ä¢ Access to 5,000+ contractors
   ‚Ä¢ Real reviews from homeowners
   ‚Ä¢ Transparent pricing"

‚ùå "Verified contractors can be gotten in minutes"
‚ùå "You will receive access to contractors"
```

### Numbers
- Write out one through nine
- Use numerals for 10+
- Use numerals for dollar amounts ($5, $1,500)
- No spaces before currency symbols

```
‚úÖ "five verified contractors"
‚úÖ "12 projects completed"
‚úÖ "$150-$250 per project"
‚ùå "5 verified contractors"
‚ùå "twelve projects"
‚ùå "150 - 250 dollars"
```

### Contractions
‚úÖ Use natural contractions (you're, we're, don't)
‚ùå Avoid in very formal contexts (legal, terms of service)

```
‚úÖ "You'll love working with verified contractors"
‚ùå "You will love working with verified contractors"
```

## Platform-Specific Voice

### Website & Marketing
**Tone:** Friendly, welcoming, inspirational
**Goal:** Convert visitors into users
**Approach:** Tell stories, highlight benefits, build excitement

**Example:**
"Tired of searching for contractors? EZLY connects you with verified professionals in your area. Post your project, get quotes, hire with confidence."

### Dashboard & Product
**Tone:** Professional, direct, action-oriented
**Goal:** Help users accomplish tasks efficiently
**Approach:** Clear instructions, minimal words, guiding language

**Example:**
"Post New Project" (button)
"Select a category below to get started"

### Email & Notifications
**Tone:** Friendly but professional
**Goal:** Keep users engaged and informed
**Approach:** Personalization, relevance, clear action

## Dos and Don'ts Summary

### DO ‚úÖ
- Use active voice
- Be clear and concise
- Use benefit-focused language
- Sound like yourself (genuine)
- Celebrate successes
- Use contractions naturally
- Put the user first
- Be honest and transparent

### DON'T ‚ùå
- Use jargon or technical terms
- Write passive sentences
- Sound corporate or stiff
- Make it about us, not the user
- Use unnecessary complexity
- Ignore tone context
- Condescend or oversimplify
- Make promises you can't keep
